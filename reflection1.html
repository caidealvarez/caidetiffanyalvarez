Reading and Written Response #1

“In Plato’s Cave” from On Photography by Susan Sontag

In the opening chapter of On Photography, Susan Sontag reflects on the power of image taking and how photography shapes the way we understand the world. Long before smartphones existed, she recognized that cameras were already everywhere and that taking photos had become a common way of experiencing life. Reading this chapter made me realize that photography is not just about documenting what we see, but about deciding what is important enough to remember.

For me, capturing images and creating graphics is deeply connected to emotion and memory. Photography allows me to freeze moments that might otherwise disappear with time. When I take a photo, I am not only capturing what is in front of me, but also how I felt in that moment. Susan Sontag explains that photographs can both preserve reality and distance us from it, and I see that in my own experience. Sometimes a photo helps me relive a memory, and other times it reminds me how quickly that moment passed.

This idea became especially personal to me when Bad Bunny released his album Debí Tirar Más Fotos, which translates to “I should have taken more photos.” The album reflects on nostalgia, memory, and the regret of not capturing moments while they were happening. It made me realize that photographs do not only show what you were looking at, but represent a specific time in your life that meant something to you at that moment. That idea deeply changed the way I think about image making.

When I look back at old photos, I don’t just see images — I remember the people I was with, the music I was listening to, and the emotions I felt during that phase of my life. Photography becomes a form of emotional evidence. Sontag’s writing helped me understand that images hold power because they allow us to return to moments that no longer exist, even if only visually.

Throughout this semester, we are asked to complete nine different projects using various tools. While each project is distinct, I see them as parts of a larger body of work. Each project builds on the last and reflects my growth as a media maker. Over time, these images and graphics will document not only what I created, but who I was at that moment in my life.

Representing the world visually right now feels especially important. Combining image and text allows me to communicate meaning more clearly and emotionally. Images alone can be misunderstood, and text alone can feel distant. Together, they allow me to express identity, memory, culture, and emotion. Photography is not just about capturing the present — it is about preserving meaning so that one day, I won’t have to say, debí tirar más fotos.
